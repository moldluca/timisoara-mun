Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 30, 2025

TimișoaraMUN is committed to protecting your personal data privacy. This privacy policy explains what data we collect, how we use it, and your rights regarding your data.

1. Information We Collect

We collect the following types of information:

  • Personal information: Name, email address, phone number, educational institution.
  • Registration information: Committee and country preferences, previous MUN experience.
  • Usage information: How you interact with our website and services.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use your information for:

  • Processing your conference registration
  • Communicating important conference information
  • Improving our services
  • Complying with legal obligations

3. Information Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal information with third parties for marketing purposes. We may share information with:

  • Our event hosting partners
  • Service providers who assist us in organizing the conference
  • Authorities, when required by law

General Data Protection Rules

Last update: May 30, 2025

1. General Provisions

1.1 Subject of the Terms and Conditions. These Terms govern participation in the event “Timișoara Model United Nations Conference 2025” ("TimișoaraMUN" or "Conference"), organized by the Association for Diplomatic Representation and Projects ("Organizer" or "Association").

1.2 Acceptance of the Terms. By completing the application form, paying the fee, or attending the Conference, each participant acknowledges that they have read, understood, and unreservedly accept these Terms and the Organizational Regulations.

1.3 Binding Character. These Terms form a legally binding agreement between participant and Organizer. Breaches may result in sanctions, including exclusion without refund.

1.4 Age and Eligibility. Participants must be between 16 and 25 years old, be enrolled in an educational institution or have graduated within the last two years. Minors may attend only with written consent from a parent or legal guardian.

2. Definitions

For the purposes of this document:

  • Participant – any registered individual (delegate, chairperson, observer, or other official role).
  • Delegate – a participant representing a country or organization in committee debates.
  • Chairperson – a participant facilitating debate within a committee.
  • Organizer – Association for Diplomatic Representation and Projects.
  • Conference – Timișoara Model United Nations Conference 2025, including all related events.
  • Rules of Procedure – the official procedures governing debates.
  • Confidential Materials – any information designated as confidential by the Organizer.

3. Registration and Participation

3.1 Registration Process. Participation requires accurate completion of the online form, submission of requested documents, full payment of the fee, acceptance of these Terms, and written confirmation from the Organizer.

3.2 Verification. The Organizer may verify provided information and request additional documentation.

3.3 Selection Criteria. Selection may consider prior MUN experience, application quality, motivation, English proficiency, and committee availability.

3.4 Confirmation. Participation is confirmed only after Organizer confirmation, receipt of all fees, and signing of the declaration on own responsibility.

3.5 Transfer of Seats. Confirmed seats cannot be transferred without written Organizer consent.

4. Fees and Payments

4.1 Fee Structure. Fees cover registration, working materials, meals, certificates, and scheduled social activities.

4.2 Payment Methods. Accepted payments include bank transfers, designated online platforms, or other Organizer-announced methods.

4.3 Payment Deadlines. Full payment must be received within the timeframe stated in the confirmation. Late or partial payments without prior approval may forfeit the reserved seat.

4.4 Refund Policy.

  • Voluntary cancellations: more than 30 days before – 90% refund; 20–30 days – 70%; 15–20 days – 40%; under 10 days – no refund. Exclusion for rule violations does not generate refunds.
  • Exceptional circumstances (with documentation): serious medical issues – 80%; visa refusal – 70%; natural disasters in origin area – 60%; death in family – 90%.
  • Transfers and substitutions: possible only with Organizer approval at least seven days prior.

4.5 Additional Fees. Participants bear costs for optional services, damages, or additional administrative needs (e.g., accommodation).

5. Processing of Personal Data

5.1 Principles. Data is processed in accordance with GDPR and applicable Romanian laws.

5.2 Data Categories. Identification, contact, educational, professional, photographic/audio/video content, prior MUN experience, relevant medical data, and financial information may be processed.

5.3 Purpose. Data supports conference organization, communication, certification, promotion, legal compliance, and improvement of future events.

5.4 Legal Grounds. Processing is based on contract performance, legitimate interest, explicit consent (where required), and legal obligations.

5.5 Participant Rights. Participants may request information, access, rectification, deletion (where applicable), restriction, and may lodge complaints with authorities.

5.6 Retention. Data is retained for up to five years unless law requires longer retention.

5.7 Data Transfer. Data may be shared with service providers, competent authorities, or official partners (with consent) as needed.

6. Conduct and Behavior of Participants

6.1 Standard of Conduct. Participants must act professionally, respectfully, diplomatically, collaboratively, and impartially.

6.2 Prohibited Behaviors. Discrimination, harassment, aggression, substance abuse, smoking in restricted areas, offensive language, disruption of sessions, and safety violations are forbidden.

6.3 Dress Code. Business formal attire is mandatory during sessions; smart casual is required for social events. Identification badges must be worn at all times.

6.4 Use of Technology. Devices must be on silent during sessions. Recording requires consent. Laptops/tablets are limited to conference use. Unauthorized recording is prohibited.

6.5 Originality. All materials must be original; plagiarism leads to exclusion and may void certificates. Sources must be cited, and Organizer may conduct originality checks.

6.6 Identity Verification. Participants affirm the accuracy of provided information and understand that falsification results in exclusion and potential legal consequences.

7. Responsibilities and Declaration on Own Responsibility

7.1 Mandatory Declaration. All participants must sign the Organizer’s declaration acknowledging regulations, assuming responsibility for actions, confirming voluntary participation, disclosing medical conditions, and safeguarding personal belongings.

7.2 Liability for Damages. Participants are financially responsible for damages to venues, Organizer property, third-party property, and any related expenses.

7.3 Medical Information. Participants must inform the Organizer of relevant medical conditions, allergies, medications, or dietary requirements and carry necessary medication.

7.4 Accuracy. Participants guarantee truthfulness of information and will promptly report changes.

7.5 Legal Compliance. Romanian law, venue regulations, diplomatic norms, and Organizer policies must be followed.

8. Limitation of Liability of the Organizer

8.1 Exoneration. The Organizer is not liable for accidents, injuries, property loss, participant misconduct, or consequences of participant actions outside the official program.

8.2 Specific Limitations. No liability is assumed for medical issues, interpersonal conflicts, indirect damages, lost opportunities, or emotional distress.

8.3 Insurance. Participants are encouraged to obtain personal insurance; medical costs remain their responsibility.

8.4 Minors. Parents/guardians assume full responsibility for minors; individual supervision is not provided.

8.5 Emergencies. Organizer facilitates access to emergency services and informs family when necessary. Failure to disclose medical conditions limits liability.

8.6 Supervision. Adults are responsible for their own safety. Temporary supervisors for minors may be appointed only during emergencies.

9. Intellectual Property and Image Rights

9.1 Use of Materials. Organizer may use conference materials, speeches, and content for promotion, education, archiving, and adaptation.

9.2 Image Rights. Participation grants the Organizer the non-exclusive, perpetual right to capture, publish, and use participant images, audio, and video for official purposes.

9.3 Ownership. Conference-created documents become conference property; participants retain copyright over substantial original contributions.

9.4 Limitations. Participants may not record, reproduce, or distribute confidential materials without permission, nor claim ownership over conference formats.

9.5 Attribution. Organizer will credit contributors where appropriate and respect existing copyrights.

10. Modifications and Cancellations

10.1 Right to Modify. Organizer may adjust program, venues, format, committee topics, participant numbers, and regulations.

10.2 Notification. Major changes are communicated at least 15 days in advance; minor updates are shared promptly via email and official channels.

10.3 Cancellation. In case of event cancellation, fees (minus administrative costs) are refunded. Organizer is not liable for collateral expenses but will attempt to reschedule.

10.4 Last-Minute Changes. Emergency decisions are final; participants must monitor updates. No compensation is offered for minor inconveniences.

10.5 Adaptation. Conference may be adapted to comply with public health, governmental, technical, or safety requirements.

11. Confidentiality and Security

11.1 Confidential Information. Participants may access strategies, personal data, logistical details, and communications deemed confidential.

11.2 Obligation. Confidential data must not be disclosed outside the conference context.

11.3 Security. Organizer employs security measures; participants must safeguard devices/passwords and report incidents.

11.4 Social Media. Participants should respect privacy, obtain consent for photos, use official hashtags, and avoid content that may harm the conference.

11.5 Waiting List. Registrations operate on a first-come basis; once capacity is reached, a waiting list is used. Available spots are offered chronologically with at least seven days’ notice.

11.6 International Agreements and Visas. Invitation letters may be issued after registration. Organizer is not responsible for obtaining visas; refunds follow section 4.4.

12. Sanctions and Exclusion

12.1 Sanctions. Depending on severity, participants may receive warnings, temporary exclusions, permanent exclusion, bans from future events, or restitution requests.

12.2 Criteria. Sanctions consider violation severity, impact, intent, frequency, cooperation, and history.

12.3 Procedure. Organizer investigates, hears involved parties, consults committee chairs, and communicates the final decision.

12.4 Immediate Exclusion. Physical aggression, severe threats, sexual harassment, discrimination, prohibited substances, safety endangerment, or document forgery trigger immediate exclusion.

12.5 Financial Consequences. Exclusion does not entitle refunds; damages and administrative costs remain participant responsibility.

13. Force Majeure

13.1 Definition. Force majeure includes natural disasters, public emergencies, pandemics, armed conflicts, terrorism, strikes, or transport blockades.

13.2 Effects. Organizer may cancel, postpone, modify format, change locations, or limit participation without liability.

13.3 Communication. Participants are informed promptly about options and remedial measures.

13.4 Responsibilities. Neither party is liable for indirect damages; both cooperate to find solutions.

13.5 Recovery. Organizer strives to reschedule, offer credits, proportionally refund services not delivered, and maintain engagement with participants.

14. Dispute Resolution

14.1 Internal Resolution. Disputes should first be resolved through direct discussion, mediation by Organizer representatives, and consultation with committee chairs.

14.2 Jurisdiction. Unresolved disputes fall under Romanian courts, applying Romanian law.

14.3 Applicable Law. Romanian Civil Code, laws on associations and foundations, GDPR, and other relevant regulations apply.

14.4 Alternatives. Parties may agree to arbitration, mediation, conciliation, or other mutually accepted ADR methods.

14.5 Deadlines. Complaints may be filed during the event; post-event complaints must be submitted within 30 days. Refund requests follow section 4.4 deadlines.

15. Final Provisions

15.1 Modification. Organizer may revise these Terms with at least 15 days’ notice for major changes and will publish updates on official channels.

15.2 Validity. If a clause is deemed invalid, remaining provisions stay in effect and the Parties will agree on an equivalent valid clause.

15.3 Entire Agreement. These Terms, together with the Application Form, Organizational Regulations, declarations, and related policies constitute the entire agreement.

15.4 Communications. Official communications are made via the participant’s registered email, the official website, designated platforms, and announcements during the event.

15.5 Language. The Romanian version prevails. Translations are for information only and interpretations follow Romanian legal standards.

15.6 Entry into Force. These Terms enter into force upon participant signature and remain valid until all mutual obligations are fulfilled.

15.7 Notifications and Updates. Participants must monitor official updates; failure to do so does not remove responsibilities.

15.8 Compliance with Romanian Legislation. Participants must comply with Romanian laws throughout their stay. The Organizer cooperates with authorities when required. International participants must meet immigration requirements.

By confirming participation, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and accepted these Terms and Conditions, agree to participate at your own risk, and accept the limitations of liability outlined above.
